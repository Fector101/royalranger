IMPORTANT OF WEARING A CORRECT UNIFORM

Since 1962, the Royal Rangers uniform has been an ever-changing representation of the ministry to the boys. The uniform has had many adjustments, additions and subtractions, but it has never change what it represents.

Every athletic team in our nation has every precise standard for how and when a particular uniform is worn. They take great care in making sure that the entire team meets the standards and presents them self in a uniform fashion. You will agree, a uniformed team looks impressive when they take the field. Because their uniform is identical to the other members of the team, it is not hard to know what team they play for.

As Royal Rangers we have several options of uniform to fit our local church / outpost, district and beyond. Although there may be some wiggle room on a special uniform there are very precise guidelines for utility uniform and dress uniform. It is important that every person involve in the Royal Rangers ministry our most current guidelines when wearing our uniform.

Why concern yourself about wearing it properly? Why go to all effort to ensure all uniform meets the National uniform for wear? Because when we slide the shirt on our shoulders and Button it up, we no longer represent ourselves. We become a standard bearer of a ministry that has reached thousands of boys and girls for Christ over the years. We represent every Royal Rangers who wears that uniform across the country and beyond. And what form does that standard take? It is the Royal Rangers uniform.

  • Each patch, pin, and medallion have a place, this guide will help ensure that you always correct when you wear your Royal Rangers uniform.
  • When you wear a correctly assembled Royal Rangers uniform, you can take pride in knowing that you stand with thousands who do the same

The statement from the uniform guidebook written by our founder and first national director Johnnie Barnes still applies today.

“Wear Your Uniform Correctly and Proudly”
